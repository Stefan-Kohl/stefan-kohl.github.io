MAT 421: Introduction to Real Analysis I (Spring 2012)

Stefan Kohl

Course Description

The course MAT 421 provides an introduction to real analysis. A tentative list of course topics is as follows:

  • Definition of R (Dedekind cut), Cantor's diagonal argument.
  • Convergence of sequences and series.
  • Continuous and differentiable functions.
  • Power series, Taylor series.
  • Notions of convergence of sequences of functions.
  • Lebesgue integral, measurable functions and -sets, Cantor set.
  • Metric spaces, Banach spaces, Banach fixed-point theorem.

The exact course contents depend on the learning progress and the interests of the participants. In order to support a better understanding of the topics, it is put a strong emphasis on examples.

Literature (among many other good books on the topic):

  • William F. Trench: Introduction to Real Analysis.
    http://ramanujan.math.trinity.edu/wtrench/misc/index.shtml
  • Jiri Lebl: Basic Analysis: Introduction to Real Analysis.
    http://www.jirka.org/ra/
